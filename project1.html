This project focused on modelling and mapping the Levelized Cost of Electricity (LCOE) for potential offshore wind turbine installations along the coasts. LCOE is a standardized metric that helps determine the economic viability of energy projects by calculating the average cost per unit of electricity generated over a project's lifetime.

Methodology

The analysis incorporated several key factors:

  • Average wind speeds between 2007-2013 at 140-meters above sea level.
  • Ocean bathymetry (depth) at ~450-meter resolution.
  • Geodesic distance to the nearest power line with capacity > 100kV.
  • Best available offshore wind turbine technology costs and production capacity.
  • Marine protected areas, calculated visibility from shore due to Earth's curvature, and current offshore lease sites.
  • Maps were created in ArcGIS Pro, and LCOE calculations were conducted using Python.

Key Findings

The analysis revealed several optimal locations for offshore wind development, with particularly promising areas along the northeastern coast where consistent wind patterns and moderate depths create favorable conditions for wind energy generation. The study also identified potential challenges related to grid connection and environmental considerations that would need to be addressed in future development phases.

Tools Used

This project utilized Geographic Information Systems (GIS) software for spatial analysis and visualization, along with custom Python scripts for data processing and LCOE calculations. The final map was created using ArcGIS Pro, incorporating multiple data layers to provide a comprehensive view of potential offshore wind development areas.
